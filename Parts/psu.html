PSU

The Power Supply Unit (PSU) converts mains AC to low-voltage regulated DC power for the internal components of a desktop computer. It provides electrical power to run each individual computer components such as the Motherboard for each component to execute accordingly.

Trouble Shooting Guide

Problem 1: PC won't power on

Test PSU with paperclip method.

Check wall outlet and surge protector.

Verify all cables connected.

Swap with known good PSU.

Problem 2: Random shutdowns

Check wattage capacity vs. system load.

Inspect cables for damage.

Monitor voltages with HWInfo.

Replace PSU if unstable.

Problem 3: Burning smell or noise

Power off immediately.

Inspect for dust buildup.

Check fan operation.

Replace PSU — do not continue use.
